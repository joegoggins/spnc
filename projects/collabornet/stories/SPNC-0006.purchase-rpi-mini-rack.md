## Why

SPNC-0005 needs a clean home for the dev + prod RPi 5s, with room to grow to ~5
nodes and a switch. A 10" mini rack ("homelab standard", per the geerlingguy
mini-rack project) is the right form factor: it scales to 5, holds NVMe-backed
Pi trays plus a switch, and is a properly Solarpunk way to run hardware you
control. This story is just the **buy** — assembly/provisioning lives in SPNC-0005.

## How

**Form factor: Option B — DeskPi RackMate 10" cabinet + 2U Pi 5 NVMe trays + a small switch.**

- Start with **2 Pis** (dev + prod), wired for **5** at full build.
- **NVMe over SD card** for the nodes — better I/O and endurance for a 24/7 site.
  NAS still handles backups (per SPNC-0005). Each Pi runs the NVMe HAT + the
  official Active Cooler — the standard, well-supported Pi 5 stack.
- **Power = USB-C per Pi.** One official 27W (5V/5A) supply per node into a rack
  PDU/strip. Simple and no HAT conflicts. (PoE was considered and dropped — the
  Pi 5 PoE HAT fights the NVMe HAT + cooler for the same space; not worth the risk.)
- **Anti-Amazon sourcing**: everything below is available direct from DeskPi,
  PiShop.us (US authorized RPi reseller), Adafruit, or Micro Center. No Amazon
  required — expect to pay a little more, that's the point.
